Reflections & Career Growth

Transitioning from 24 years of military service into the civilian IT sector has sharpened my appreciation for structured learning and deliberate skill-building. Looking back, the communications and cryptographic work I did in the CAF gave me a strong foundation in systems thinking, security discipline, and operational resilience — skills that translate directly into modern IT roles in infrastructure, security, and development.

The technologies I work with now — Node.js, Rust, Python, SQL, C# — are not just academic exercises. Each project is designed to solve a real problem and demonstrate the ability to move from concept to deployed solution. The S.I.R.E. platform proved I can architect a backend system under real constraints. Esoteric Wisdom pushed me to learn Rust from scratch and ship something live. These experiences are shaping how I approach system design, API security, and user-facing software.

Where I am heading: My immediate goal is a summer role in IT support, managed services, or junior development where I can contribute on day one while continuing to grow. Over the next two to three years, I aim to specialize in either full-stack backend development or security-focused systems administration — bridging my military COMSEC background with modern DevSecOps practices. Longer term, I want to contribute to public-sector or defence-adjacent technology projects where operational reliability and security posture are mission-critical.

Technology focus: I am actively deepening my knowledge of Rust for systems and backend work, expanding my Python automation skills, and pursuing Microsoft 365 certification to complement my practical experience with enterprise environments. Cloud deployment and CI/CD pipelines — developed through the S.I.R.E. project — are areas I intend to formalize through hands-on work and certification.
